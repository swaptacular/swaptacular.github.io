# Script for the video


In our imaginary town, there are three small businesses: a
construction company, a grocery store, and an automobile repair shop.

Each business has just one worker — Joe, Alice, and Bob.

Joe works in housing construction and is paid once a month.

Half of his salary comes in his employer’s digital currency: deposited
directly into his digital wallet.

Joe does not have a direct use for his employer’s currency, so he
decides to exchange it for the currencies issued by the other
businesses in town.

The same goes for Alice… and for Bob.

All three workers submit their exchange requests to a specialized
server on the Internet.

And this is where the magic happens.

Because all the currencies are pegged to the national currency, the
server can automatically calculate how to swap them — so that everyone
gets exactly what they need.

In the end, Joe receives currency from the grocery store, and from the
automobile repair shop.

Alice and Bob receive the currencies they need as well.

During the month, Joe can buy groceries with the grocery store’s
currency, and pay for car repairs with the repair shop’s currency.

Alice and Bob do the same.

Each month, the process repeats: workers receive their salaries,
submit their exchange requests, and obtain the currencies they need…
for their purchases throughout the month. All of this is completely
automated.

In a real town or city, thousands of businesses and workers can
participate. The server automatically matches exchange requests so
that as many people as possible get what they want — efficiently and
fairly.
